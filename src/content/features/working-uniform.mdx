I got into clothes late — about a year ago. The reason was unromantic: my friend kept dressing better than me, and I started paying attention to why. What I found was that the people whose fits I liked best weren't buying *more*; they were buying *more carefully*.

## the rule I gave myself

One piece a month, maximum. Either second-hand or from a brand I actually know something about. No impulse buys, no trend pieces I'll be embarrassed by in a year. The point is to assemble a wardrobe slowly enough that I have to live with each piece before adding to it.

## what's earned a spot

A Stüssy basic tee in cream — the kind you stop noticing because it just works. Carhartt WIP double-knees that look better the more they fade. A pair of New Balance 2002R in a quiet grey that pretty much anything else in the rotation can sit on top of. A vintage Filson cap I found in Tokyo for less than dinner.

## what I keep almost buying

Engineered Garments shirts. Yohji wide trousers (definitely too much commitment, definitely will own a pair eventually). One specific Aimé Leon Dore hoodie that I've been talking myself out of for six weeks.

## the through-line

Streetwear is a doorway, not a room. What I'm actually drawn to is *functional, slightly oversized, slightly worn-looking*. That description fits a Carhartt jacket and an Engineered Garments shirt and an old Filson bag, and probably nothing currently on the wall at H&M.

I'll know I'm getting somewhere when I stop having to think about what to put on.
